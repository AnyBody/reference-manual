AnyForceSurfaceContact¶

Type

Creatable

Parent class

AnyAppliedForce

Description¶

This class function calculates contact forces and moments between two STL surfaces (master and slave) and applies these to the reference frames of the surfaces.

The contact force between two objects represented by stl surfaces is calculated using a linear force law in the penetration volume (e.g. volume of the intersection of surface geometries)

\[F_c = P \cdot V_p\]

where \(P\) is the constant PressureModule defined for the contact object.

For the calculation of the contact force, the penetration depth of a vertex \(d_v\) is computed as distance to the closest point on the opponent surface and is marked as being in contact if the penetration depth is between MinDepth and MaxDepth. For a vertex in contact, a contact force is calculated using two possible computations

  • If no depth function as AnyParamFun is defined for the AnyForceSurfaceContact object the force is calculated as

    \[F_v = P A_v d_v n_v\]

    with the vertex area \(A_v\) defined as a third of the sum of all adjacent triangles and the vertex normal \(n_v\).

  • If a depth function \(f(x)\) defined as AnyParamFun in the object, the contact force is calculated as

    \[F_v = A_v f(d_v) n_v\]

In both cases, the moment is calculated using the moment arm \(r_v\) as vector between vertex and reference frame of the surface as

\[M_v = r_v \times F_v\]

The contact force and moment between the surfaces is calculated as the sum of vertex forces and moments

\[F = \sum F_v\]

and

\[M = \sum M_v,\]

respectively.

Additional to force and moment, the center of pressure (COP) is calculated as average of position vectors of penetrating vertices \(p_v\) using the same weight function

\[\mathrm{COP} = \sum p_v A_v f(d_v),\]

the contact area as

\[\mathrm{ContactArea} = \sum A_v\]

and the maximal penetration depth as

\[\mathrm{MaxPenetration} = \max d_v\]

In case of a double sided contact calculation (SingleSidedOnOff set to ‘Off’), a contact force is calculated for the pairing from master to slave surface and from slave to master surface and the contact force, moment, contact area and the COP is computed as the average, the MaxPenetration is the maximum of the maximal penetration of both surface pairings.

In the member MCOP, the torque applied to the center of pressure is computed such that in the point COP the moment equilibrium

\[M - ( (\mathrm{COP} - r) \times F ) + MCOP = 0\]

with with moment arm vector from location of reference frame to COP \(r-COP\) holds.

Force and moment vectors in global coordinates are calculated for the reference frames of master and slave reference frames in Fmaster, Mmaster and Fslave, Mslave respectively. These values are also output in Fout in the above mentioned order.

To improve the accuracy of the volume calculation, a planar mesh refinement subdividing a triangle by cutting an edge in the middle and refine with four triangles can be used. The members MeshRefinementMaster and MeshRefinementSlave define the level of refinement, i.e. how often this refinement algorithm is applied to the STL surface.

The vertex contact forces can be visualized using an AnyDrawForceSurface object.

To obtain good results for the contact forces, a number of quality requirements concerning the used geometries should be fullfilled.

  • The surface should not have sharp edges in the region of contact, which could cause jumps in the calculation of the penetration depth

    or big changes in force directions. In combination with a FDK solver, this might cause problems.

  • The surface should not have too large curvatures. This could cause the same problems as sharp edges.

  • The surface should not contain too small triangles.

    Small triangles, i.e. vertices which are too close to each other are collapsed into one vertex during the process of converting STL-files to AnySurf objects. Thus, too small triangles (edge length < 0.0001) end up as degenerated triangles with edges of zero length and without area. These triangles are ignored in the contact calculation and warnings show up.

  • The face normal for each face of the surface should have a unique orientation.

    Make sure that the surface normal and the numbering of triangles (best counter clockwise for a consistent definition of the outer face normal) are consistent. The normal is used to define inside and outside of the surface and thus, which direction is penetration.

ProjectionThresholdMaster and ProjectionThresholdSlave are thresholds for the projections of the connection lines (lines between a contact point and its pair on the opposing surface) and the surface normal (here the master surface) for accepting a contact condition. Essentially, it is the cosine to the angle (the dot product) and it can be used to remove poor contact pairs due to e.g. sharp surface edges.
